Project: Save The Princess
Course: Computer Science Principles
Duration: 8/30/18 - 9/7/18
Objective: Make a Multistage Adventure Game
Type of Project: Group Project

Our goal for this project was to make a multi stage adventure game using Scratch that would consist of intricate puzzles and skill to complete. Before designing stage, we brainstormed countless ideas and chose the most challenging option to test our limits. We were helped by neighboring teams, who aided us in brainstorming. We then proceeded to develop the idea further, including more detailed and precise stages. Our main goal however, was to design a game where every level is a new experience and concept, totally unique and harder compared to the last. Our game consists of a multitude of sprites, including bats, lions, foxes, frogs, and even a witch. The mechanics of the game include shooting arrows, lighting, and more. While in the midst of coding this project, I ran into quite a few roadblocks. Things I haven’t learned or have ever heard of, however this didn’t stop my partner and I. We delegated our work, searching the internet, watching tutorials, all to figure out how to make a small action happen, like a slight animation. Through research and learning we were able to come up with a system that counts time and health, we were able to create bosses with animations that would follow you. We made interactive gameplay where every action you do matters. My partner did most of the documentation and helped brainstorm, giving me his input and helping with the code when I was struggling. I decided to take a shot at coding. I feel like we worked to our best potential.

Save The Gorillas
Course: Computer Science Principles
Duration: 3/4/19 - 3/25/19
Objective: Edit an Image to Support a Cause
Type of Project: Group Project

Our algorithm uses a combination of masking, geometrical shapes/abstract art, human interaction, the ability to manipulate multiple different parameters, superimposed logos and borders, and extra features to make our image manipulations even more memorable. Some strengths of our algorithm are that it meets all of the client’s criteria, it has simple and efficient user interactions, and can be easily reused. We included user input to make you able to edit each component of the image which consists of the border, icon and background. In the border, you can change the thickness and color to anything you desire. In the icon, you are able to choose the color and size to anything but you can also choose the icon location. The locations are Top-Right, Top-Left, Bottom-Left, and Bottom-Right. In the background, you can choose either polka dots or solid background. If you choose polka dots, you can choose the color for both the polka dots and the background behind them. If you choose background, you choose a color to set it to. We worked together pretty well and brainstormed good ideas and how to properly execute them. We were able to code to what I think was our best possible ability. We had a few differences which were bound to happen with multiple people editing the code. We both spent a good amount of time researching and telling each other what we plan to do. Documentation was a daily habit and I learned how to search and find the information necessary to program such a complex task I had never done before. This was by far the most challenging project I have done this year and surpassed the amount of hours I spent on other projects. I think this really brought out the hard working and focused state of mind from within me.
